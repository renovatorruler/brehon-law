## THE PREMISE
Near-now. A harmonization administration — elected, commie-captured, named on
the page (domestic fellow-travelers + the People's Bloc holding the debt) —
signs the **Lunar Heritage Neutralization Accord**: in exchange for debt
restructuring and "a new era of planetary partnership," the United States will
retrieve the six Apollo flags and "contextualize" Tranquility Base — artifacts
crated for a UN museum, Unity markers planted in their place. The private
launch fleet has been nationalized to do it. The de-flagging is scheduled as
the global broadcast centerpiece of **"Unity Day" — July 4th, the 250th** —
the birthday cancelled on Earth and erased on the Moon in one telecast.
THE INSULT THAT POWERS THE MOVIE: our rockets, our pads, our taxes, our flags.

## THE COUNTER-FORCE (Armageddon rule: the wrong people are the only people)
The government seized the fleet — but not what it had already written off:
- **THE SHIP: "LIBERTY BELL."** The founder's first-generation prototype,
  grounded forever for a weld flaw and scrapped to a Texas boneyard. The
  cracked bell that they said could never ring. She flies anyway. (The flaw
  is load-bearing: the crack IS the thesis in hardware.)
- **THE CREW (ragtag, per the law):**
  - THE CANCELLED ASTRONAUT — trained for the Apollo mission that was cut in
    '72; the man history erased once already; near-80, meanest sim scores
    ever recorded; been waiting 50 years for the phone to ring.
  - THE FIRED CHIEF ENGINEER — canned in the nationalization for refusing to
    sign the prototype's death certificate. The Bell is hers. She knows the
    crack personally.
  - THE BARNSTORMER — suborbital stunt pilot, drag-strip energy, has never
    once filed a flight plan.
  - THE WELDER / THE CRANE OPERATOR — Armageddon texture; the hands.
  - THE FOUNDER — in exile after the seizure; never appears on the ship; just
    leaves the boneyard gate unlocked and mails somebody the keys. (Fictional
    flavor, kept offscreen — a wink, not a cameo.)

## THE WEAPON (the Hamilton note: pranks, not battles)
They cannot outgun a government. So they don't fight — they get there FIRST
and make solemn tyranny RIDICULOUS on the biggest broadcast in human history.
The world tunes in to watch the flags come down — and finds six Americans in
mismatched suits already sitting at Tranquility Base in lawn chairs, having
the most illegal cookout ever staged, doing donuts in a restored rover, and
raising a 51st flag next to the original six. You cannot wrestle old men off
the Moon on live TV on America's 250th birthday. The Accord doesn't die in a
firefight; it dies of laughter. (Sod, still held: shame is the weapon;
shamelessness is the shield — now played OFFENSIVELY.)
