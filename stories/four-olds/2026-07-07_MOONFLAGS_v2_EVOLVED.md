## THE PLAYERS

prince of a coastal machine; wins the Nobel Peace Prize for ANNOUNCING the
Accord (prize first, deed never — the Obama-prize gag played straight). His
power is image: adored abroad, untouchable at home. That image is also his
wall — you cannot arrest grandfathers on the Moon on live TV and keep it.
**MAGNUS HALE** — founder of Frontier (rockets, cars, the platform). The
administration's hostage: comply, or nationalization "in the public
interest." 100,000 employees' jobs held over his head. Publicly he signs.
Publicly he grovels. The world calls him a sellout for two whole acts —
while a boneyard gate in Texas quietly goes unlocked. His detonation is
saved for Act 3, on live TV, when they demand he bring the crew down:
**"You want to nationalize me? Go. Fuck. Yourself."** (The DealBook echo,
placed at the exact moment the world is already laughing.)
**THE HARMONY CREW** — the official mission: two solemn ESA career
astronauts + one People's Bloc observer, sent on Frontier's nationalized
fleet to retrieve the six flags "with dignity" and raise the **Harmony
Banner** — a rainbow-gradient globe designed by a 44-nation committee (four
years, three vetoes, beige consensus). They are professionals, not villains
— which makes their assignment the joke and their embarrassment the crack
where the laughter gets in.

## WHY RETIREES (the canon answer, four layers deep)
1. **Legal:** Frontier operates under a federal Compliance Monitorship —
   every badge logged, every packet audited. One employee fingerprint on a
   rogue launch = instant nationalization + prison for kids with mortgages.
   Hale cannot use a single person on payroll.
2. **Technical (the beautiful one):** the modern fleet is fly-by-cloud —
   every ship phones home through the federally monitored mission net. You
   cannot fly one dark. But the LIBERTY BELL — Hale's scrapped first
   prototype, grounded forever for a weld flaw — is pre-network: analog
   gauges, stick-and-rudder, celestial navigation backup. Only the old breed
   ever flew hands-on. THE OLD SKILLS ARE THE ONLY ONES THAT CAN'T BE
   CONFISCATED. (Old ideas, old culture, old customs, old habits — the Four
   Olds save the day BECAUSE they're old. Mao's list, inverted into a crew
   manifest.)
3. **Deniability:** retirees and civilians. Hale's shrug on the record: "I
   lose track of scrap all the time."
4. **Leverage-proof:** you can't threaten men who already gave everything.
   "What are they gonna do — cancel my Medicare on the Moon?"

## STRUCTURE (race spine, prank finale)
**ACT 1 — THE ANNOUNCEMENT.** Marwani's Nobel montage; the Accord signing;
the Harmony Banner unveiled to a standing ovation in Geneva; Hale's public
capitulation (the world spits on him); the de-flagging scheduled as the
global centerpiece of UNITY DAY — July 4th, the 250th. Intimate atrocity to
close the act: the Smithsonian's Apollo gallery "decommissioned" on camera —
Cricket watches his cancelled mission's capsule crated for "contextualized
storage," and the phone in his barn finally rings.
**ACT 2 — THE BONEYARD.** The build, played as heist-Americana: the Bell
under thirty years of tarps; machine shops in three states making parts on
wrong paperwork; Mission Control in the back office of a bass-boat
dealership; Mama Bear re-welding her own crack by hand. Intercut with the
Harmony crew's solemn training (mirror-montage comedy). The Bell launches
dark from a mothballed Matagorda pad at 2 AM — the kids fly, the olds call
the numbers, and the world doesn't know for eleven minutes.
**ACT 3 — TRANQUILITY.** They land FIRST. They set the stage: lawn chairs,
the red-white-blue umbrella, the resistor-plate grill (Gunny insists it
counts), rover donuts cutting fresh tracks around the sacred six — which
they do NOT touch. They guard them. The 51st goes up: ROOM FOR ONE MORE.
The Harmony lander arrives to the most illegal Fourth of July cookout in
human history, live on every screen on Earth. The standoff is a comedy the
president cannot win: pranks, not punches — the solemn banner ceremony
upstaged, the Bloc observer fuming, one ESA astronaut cracking on a hot mic:
"...is that a burger?" On Earth: Marwani demands Hale end it; Hale's
televised **go-fuck-yourself**; the laughter goes planetary; allies peel;
the Nobel becomes a punchline; the Accord dies of ridicule. The Euros, to
their credit, accept a burger for the photo before they withdraw. Over the
open loop, to the whole listening Earth: **"It's Independence Day."** Beat.
**"God bless America."** WHITE OUT on a flashbulb — no, on the fireworks
Tito smuggled: three shells, fired into a black sky where they burn strange
and perfect with no air to slow them.
**EPILOGUE:** Tranquility declared a National Historic Park (the crew were,
technically, the only legal party present — the preservation law was on
THEIR side all along); the Bell never comes home — she's the park's first
exhibit, THE FOUR OLDS still painted on her hull, visible from the new
telescopes on a clear night.
