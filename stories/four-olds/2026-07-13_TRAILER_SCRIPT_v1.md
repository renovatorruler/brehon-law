# THE FOUR OLDS — theatrical trailer script (v1, 2:28)

LAW: the cookout is never shown (the film's one big withhold). The trailer may
show the launch, the empty flag sites, and the standoff silhouettes — never
lawn chairs, never the grill. Flag colors are never adjudicated on screen.
All dialogue is verbatim from v14.2. Music cues describe type, never artist.

---

**0:00 — THE WORLD (soft open, ~35s)**

MUSIC: a lone pedal-steel figure, warm and patient — Sunday-morning Americana.

- Green band card.
- WIDE: monitors flip the electoral board red to blue in one second. Balloons.
- MARWANI (at the podium, arena huge): "On behalf of the United States, to
  every nation we have wronged — we are sorry."
- Diner. The counter radio, tinny: "...the sale, use, and private storage of
  consumer fireworks is prohibited nationwide—"
- BUCK, feed cap, turns his cup a quarter on the saucer: "Huh."
- CARD: **THIS JULY 4TH**
- ANCHOR (V.O., broadcast): the Accord — the six Apollo flags come down, one
  to each of the world's great regions. IMAGE: the artist's rendering, a flag
  boiled white.

**0:35 — THE MEN (the turn begins, ~30s)**

MUSIC: the pedal steel picks up a snare, quiet march.

- The barn. Four old men at a plywood trainer. GUNNY, formal: "Apollo
  Eighteen. Lunar descent rehearsal. Number two thousand, eight hundred and
  six."
- CARD: **THE BEST WHO NEVER GOT TO PROVE IT**
- The kangaroo court, fast: STITCH: "Machine landed it. He rode it down." /
  DUTCH: "Three hundred and eighty. I took it off the loop myself."
- The seizure: Cricket plants himself in the barn doorway, feet set. "You'll
  have to move me, Wade." — and Wade, gently, moves him. Hold on Cricket's
  face, half a beat too long.
- The worksheet on the kitchen table, a child's pencil: THE 4TH OF JULY
  COOKOUT AT GRANDPAS.

**1:05 — THE CAPER (needle drop, ~40s)**

MUSIC: HARD CUT IN — a 1960s AM-radio country-rock stomper, sunny and
defiant. The cutting doubles its pace.

- The auction barn: sodium lamps BANG on, bank after bank.
- MACK deals contract packets: the job — build the crates that bring the
  flags home.
- DUTCH, tape measure inside a crate: "Interior's awful generous for cloth."
- TITO, not looking up: "Man could about fit in there."
- SMASH TO BLACK. One breath of silence.
- Cricket sits on the crate edge and unlaces his boots. "Somebody time me
  getting in. We'll want a number."
- MUSIC BACK, bigger: the build montage — soda lime into canisters, welding
  bottles on a pallet, the chalk outline, suits coming out of scrap crates,
  Stitch's needle and thread.
- The weigh-in. The dockworker's clipboard at LOT D. A whisper: "All...
  clear."
- LAUNCH — the pad rumble swallows the music for two seconds. On the
  booster's climb, the white primer BURNS AWAY in sheets — the flag
  underneath, on every screen on Earth.
- WHITE HOUSE LAWYER, appalled: "The only Americans on the Moon are the ones
  you want removed."

**1:45 — THE STANDOFF (tease, never the party, ~25s)**

MUSIC: drops to a single sustained note under sound design.

- The retrieval tour: helmet lights on an EMPTY flag site. A bare pole. Then
  another.
- A STREAMER, veins out, delighted: the hoax corner detonating.
- Helmet-cam, grainy: SIX FLAGS standing in a ring. Four suited silhouettes
  step in front of them, line abreast. (No grill. No chairs. Silhouettes and
  colors only.)
- GUNNY (suit radio, flat): "You want 'em? Come through us."
- Rapid stabs: Vess sliding a signature log across a desk — "Count the
  signatures and tell me whose." / Hale at a podium, jaw set: "Go. [BLEEP].
  Yourself." / a porch light clicking on, then a whole street of them, then a
  coastline rolling west.

**2:10 — TITLE + BUTTON (~18s)**

- SILENCE. Four crate stencils slam on in sequence: OLD IDEAS. OLD CULTURE.
  OLD CUSTOMS. OLD HABITS.
- The stencils wipe to the TITLE CARD: **THE FOUR OLDS**
- MUSIC: one last chorus hit, then out.
- BUTTON (black, then the shot): the commander's helmet fills the frame.
  BRANDT: "Where did you come from?"
  STITCH (dead straight): "Left us up here in sixty-seven. Somebody had to
  see to the sites."
  DUTCH (off, immediate): "Seventy-two."
  STITCH: "Rounding."
- CARD: **INDEPENDENCE DAY. FOR REAL THIS TIME.** *(alt: JULY 4TH — date card
  only)*

---

Production notes: the mail line is deliberately absent (protected for the
film); the cookout, the burger handoff, Adams's letter, the Moon fireworks,
and the ramp sacrifice are all withheld. The trailer's last information is
"four old men are standing between the world and the flags" — the theater
buys what happens next.
