## 1 · THE MOVIE IN ONE BREATH

**Logline:** A commie president wins the White House, humiliates America into
apologizing to the world, and — as the final gesture of a "kinder" United
States — sends an international crew on nationalized American rockets to
remove the six flags from the Moon and gift them to the nations America has
wronged. Four retired backup astronauts — the men NASA trained for the Moon
and never sent — smuggle themselves aboard in museum crates and defend the
flags the only way America knows how: with the most illegal Fourth of July
cookout in human history.

**Register:** rage-and-release. Make America furious for 30 minutes. Let it
conspire for an hour. Let it erupt for the rest. Nobody mourns anything at any
point. The finale must goosebump twice — the wall, then the lights.

**The two laws (non-negotiable):**
1. Commies in it, NAMED on the page — or it's commie gobbledygook.
2. The greatness of America in it — or it's commie gobbledygook.

**Comedy direction:** every joke lands on the apparatus. Never on the flags,
never on the anthem, never on the men.

**The Sod (held, never stated by anyone, ever):** shame is the weapon;
shamelessness is the shield. The erased kept the faith.

**Title logic:** the regime's propaganda brands the crew "four obsolete men."
Mao's own list — old ideas, old culture, old customs, old habits — is taught
admiringly by the regime inside the film, and it's the old ideas, old culture,
old customs, and old habits that beat them. Tito stencils the four
people-crates with the four olds as fake stock labels; the world reads the
crate lids on camera at the reveal.

## 2 · THE WORLD

**PRESIDENT MARWANI, 41.** DSA-affiliated, youngest president ever, adored
abroad — wins the Nobel Peace Prize for ANNOUNCING his agenda. Governs by
image and consensus. His wall: an image president cannot arrest grandfathers
on the Moon on live television; and the People's Bloc holds his debt and his
leash. RESOLUTION (mine, overrulable): Marwani appears ONLY in public/broadcast
footage — podiums, PSAs, addresses, one voice-call relayed to the ship. No
private Oval scenes, no soft-side beats. The regime is experienced the way
ordinary Americans experience it: on screens, on letterhead, and as an
inspector at the door.

**THE ACCORD.** The Lunar Heritage Neutralization Accord: an international
delegation retrieves the six Apollo flags — "bleached white by fifty years of
radiation; white flags; flags of surrender; let us retire them with dignity" —
and returns them to Earth to be GIFTED, one each, to nations America has
wronged (Iran, Latin America, and so on), replaced on the Moon by the Harmony
Banner (a rainbow-gradient globe designed by a 44-nation committee: four
years, three vetoes). The retrieval broadcast is scheduled as the centerpiece
of UNITY DAY — July 4th. NASA can no longer reach the Moon; only Frontier can.
The threat: fly the mission or be destroyed — contracts pulled, permits
frozen, nationalization "in the public interest."

**FRONTIER.** Magnus Hale's rocket company — the only ride to the Moon on
Earth. Under a Federal Compliance Monitorship: every badge logged, every
manifest audited, a government minder in every meeting. The American flag on
the booster is ordered painted over for the mission.

**THE COUNTRY.** A character with an arc, played by recurring faces: THE
DINER (the same booths, the same coffee, the regulars we return to at every
regime beat — slumped lower, jaw tighter) and THE SHOP FLOOR. The country
goes: silent → simmering → quietly refusing → erupting. Street-level takings
under the new regime, planted early, paid at the cookout: **fireworks banned
federally** (carbon targets — background radio in the cold open), property
taxes doubled, the anthem "retired" at ballgames, backyard grills metered for
carbon compliance. Everything banned down here is on the table up there.

## 3 · THE PEOPLE (locked names)

### The Four Olds (the crates)
- **CMDR. EARL "CRICKET" DAWES, 79** — THE LEAD; the movie rides his eyes.
  Backup commander for the last Apollo mission: trained for the Moon, never
  flew, never even processed out — the GHOST FILE: a $0.00 pay stub,
  quarterly, for fifty years ("DAWES, E. — ASTRONAUT (ACTIVE)"). Keeps
  current in a homebuilt barn simulator; fifty years of Tuesday-night logbooks.
  Broke: the new property assessments have him at the bank asking for
  extensions. Flaw: he stopped believing anyone would ever ask. Arc: private
  kept faith → public joyful defiance. He says the last lines.
- **WALLACE "DUTCH" HAAS, 76** — the graybeard engineer. Apollo-era systems
  man, then one of Hale's first hires when Frontier was a junkyard with a
  dream; wrote half the cargo standards himself, including the Class-A
  archival containment spec; pushed out in the "safety-culture modernization."
  Owns the binder. Administers the 1968 flight physical, because that's the
  last standard that ever mattered.
- **COL. ELMORE "STITCH" McGREW, 74** — SR-71, then shuttle. The
  stick-and-rudder man. Flew everything, filed nothing. Trains the kids'
  hands; re-checks the computer with a slide rule.
- **GUNNERY SGT. LEO "GUNNY" TIBBS, 71** — USMC pad crew chief; bossed the
  white room while officers borrowed his machine. Owner of the flag protocol
  and the grill, both sacraments. Keeper of crates five and six. Reads the
  Adams letter as grace.

### The Ground Crew
- **MACK BOONE, 55** — Frontier cargo-integration foreman; the inside man who
  brings the ask to the Legion hall. Retroactively revealed as Vess's agent.
- **JOSS REED, 26** — suborbital stunt racer working Frontier's loading dock;
  drives the crates; runs the covert ham link. Never filed a flight plan.
- **TITO ALVAREZ, 24** — fabricator prodigy from the "restructured" (fired)
  shop; welds the crates; stencils the labels; smuggles three firework shells;
  his grandmother **LITA** sews the 51-star flag at her kitchen table, unasked.
- **DANNY DAWES, ~50** — Cricket's son. Delivers the flag news by phone ("Dad.
  Maybe don't watch the news."). Figures out the barn. His whole enlistment:
  "You need a driver?"
- **PEG DAWES, 77** — Cricket's wife. Packs his kit like it's 1972. Fifty
  years of knowing exactly who she married.

### Frontier's Towers
- **MAGNUS HALE** — founder. The administration's hostage: complies, grovels,
  reads their statements like a hostage tape; the world calls him a sellout
  for two acts. Chooses not to see (the audience never watches him plan
  anything). Detonates in Act 3, live, when ordered to bring the crew down:
  **"You want to nationalize me? Go. Fuck. Yourself."** Then one post on the
  platform he still owns: **"Look up."**
- **MIRA VESS** — CEO of Frontier and mother of three of Hale's children; the
  press sneer they can't categorize. PLAYED AS HATED FOR ~90 MINUTES: she
  enforces compliance, orders the booster flag painted over, fires 200
  workers, donates the lobby's Apollo relics to "contextualization," testifies
  AGAINST Hale. THE REVEAL (Act 3): she was the mastermind — the firings
  built the off-books crate shop, the auction put the simulator back in the
  barn stamped SCRAP, the testimony kept a federal receiver out of the
  building, Mack was hers, the Monitor's inspections were scheduled by her.
  Norton's-safe scene: she lays the Monitor's own signatures in front of him.
  "You wanted the company to cooperate. The company cooperated. The country
  didn't." Every hate-scene is double-written and re-reads as protection.
- **ADMINISTRATOR PELL** — the Federal Harmony Monitor; the movie's warden.
  Petty, smug, laminated harmony quotes. Repossesses the simulator (riding a
  county tax seizure). Gets ONE real win (the crate weigh-in). His safe
  moment: he personally inspected and signed crate four. Twice.

### The Harmony Crew (not villains — professionals on the wrong side)
- **CMDR. BRANDT** (ESA) — solemn, decent; the hot-mic "...is that a burger?"
  is his; accepts the burger for the photograph.
- **MAJOR LINDQVIST** (ESA) — the second chair.
- **OBSERVER SHEN** (People's Bloc) — the political officer; palm on crate
  four; fumes in his visor at the party.

## 4 · WHY RETIREES (the canon logic, stated once in-film by Mack)
1. **Legal:** the Monitorship logs every employee; one payroll fingerprint on
   the plot = nationalization + prison for kids with mortgages. Zero
   employees may touch it.
2. **Technical:** the modern fleet is fly-by-cloud on the monitored mission
   net. But the mission rides hardware built to DUTCH'S OLD SPEC, and the
   crates are pre-network, analog, self-contained. Only the old breed ever
   ran hands-on systems. THE OLD SKILLS ARE THE ONLY SKILLS THAT CAN'T BE
   CONFISCATED.
3. **Deniability:** retirees, no employment ties. "I lose track of scrap."
4. **Leverage-proof:** "What are they gonna do — cancel my Medicare on the
   Moon?"

## 5 · THE PLAN
The retrieval mission must bring six flags home; the manifest therefore
carries six museum-grade Class-A archival containers — climate-controlled,
nitrogen-purged, independently powered. A preservation crate is a
life-support system with a museum sticker on it. Dutch wrote the spec; Tito
welds them in the off-books shop staffed by Vess's "fired" 200; Mack loads
them; four carry old men, two carry the party. The playbook is 1775, chosen
out loud — handwritten letters between shops, a Reader's Digest book cipher,
the ham net, Kodachrome in a coffee can — because nobody alive fights like
1775 and no algorithm reads a knock, a knot, or a slide rule. Gunny: "They've
got every algorithm ever written. We've got Valley Forge."

## 6 · KNOWLEDGE DESIGN (what's withheld, what's honest)
- **No cheating, ever:** crates, loading, inspection, launch, landing — all
  shown honestly. Amaze with withheld MEANING, not hidden mechanics.
- **THE COOKOUT IS THE ONE BIG WITHHOLD.** The audience knows four men are
  riding to the Moon to defend the flags; it must NOT know about the party.
  Crates five and six are "Gunny's gear." The plan-briefing scene cuts away
  at "Gunny — show 'em." On the surface we watch them slip out and take the
  backup rover — then we do NOT see what they do. Production note: the
  cookout stays out of every trailer.
- **THE VESS REVEAL** holds until Act 3. Every prior scene double-written.
- **The ghost file** plants as a bank-scene joke (the $0.00 stub) and fires
  at the standoff: the olds are, on paper, the only active-duty American
  astronauts on site.
- **The fireworks ban** plants as background radio in the cold open and
  fires as Tito's three shells over the Sea of Tranquility.
- **The white-flag lie** plants in regime propaganda ("bleached white — flags
  of surrender") and dies on camera at the reveal. RESOLUTION (mine,
  overrulable): the flags are VIBRANT — the colors never faded. Honest
  science on call if ever challenged (no wind: furled layers self-shield;
  regolith dust is opaque sunscreen; vacuum = no oxygen, moisture, or rot;
  Apollo 11's flag fell face-down in '69 and kept its dye in the dark). The
  regime asserted decay from theory; free men went and looked.
- **Marwani = screens only.** Pell and Vess and the Harmony crew are flesh.

## 7 · THE MOVIE, END TO END (minute map)

### ACT 1 — THE TAKING (0–27) · make America furious
**Cold open montage (~4 min, no protagonist):** CNN election night → the
commentator ("the first DSA-affiliated nominee to win the presidency...
perhaps tonight begins the reconciliation with a world the last
administration spent years insulting") → inauguration: "On behalf of the
United States — to every nation we have wronged — we are sorry." → pallets
of cash on a C-17 ramp (IRAN COMPENSATION FRAMEWORK) → Brussels signing
(U.S. COMMITS $200B TO EUROPEAN DEFENSE RENEWAL) → the billionaire tax →
the Senate grilling of Hale (the word "nationalization" savored) → the
diner: cold coffee, dead silence, and under it the radio, almost inaudible:
all fireworks banned, federal carbon targets. "Huh." TITLE.
**The bank (~5):** Cricket, 79, in his good shirt, asking for an extension
on doubled property taxes. The loan officer scrolling: "You receive...
$0.00 quarterly from NASA?" — "Clerical thing. Fifty years now." (Plant.)
**The barn & the net (~5):** the homebuilt simulator; the wall of logbooks
(every Tuesday since 1972); the Tuesday net — Dutch, Stitch, Gunny as VOICES
first, three old men razzing through a checklist. We love the world before
it's taken.
**The last straws (~8):** DANNY's call: "Dad. Maybe don't watch the news."
The Accord announced: flags down, gifted to the wronged, Harmony Banner up,
July 4th broadcast. The diner watches the smug watch-parties celebrate.
Then the SUVs at the barn — the un-retire trope inverted: they've come to
TAKE, not to ask. County tax seizure + Pell's federal tag: "sensitive
aerospace training apparatus — excluded from public auction." Fifty years
onto a flatbed. They leave the logbooks: "No salvage value." Cricket in the
empty barn, a clean rectangle of dust.
**The meeting (~5) — DOORWAY 1:** Legion hall backroom. The net's voices get
faces. Dutch: "Been talking to a fella at Frontier. Hear him out." MACK
BOONE lays one photocopied page on the table — the Class-A spec — "It's a
life-support system with a museum sticker on it. Spec was written by a man
in this room." Beat. "So. You gentlemen want to go to the Moon?" Silence —
four men who've waited fifty years to be asked, none of whom can say no in
front of the others. Cricket walks out; for one breath the movie lets you
think he's leaving; he comes back with an armload of logbooks and drops
fifty years of Tuesdays on the table: **"I'm current."** Mack feeds the spec
sheet into the wood stove: "Then this meeting never happened." That night
the net carries its first coded traffic.

### ACT 2 — THE CONSPIRACY (27–90) · the joy of the fuck-you being built
**Movement 1 — THE SCHOOL OF OLD MEN (27–48).** Mack brings the kids: Joss
off the dock, Tito from the fired shop. The off-books operation stands up in
the dead auction warehouse where 200 skilled men officially don't work
(Vess's "restructuring" — hate-scene #1, double-written). Dutch runs the
1968 physical from the binder; Cricket's resting heart rate embarrasses a
smartwatch; crate endurance drills with side bets; knock-code at the kitchen
table until Peg learns it too. The simulator comes home on a flatbed stamped
SCRAP in orange (nobody questions a miracle). Boomer-craft montage: coded
ham traffic, the Reader's Digest cipher, Kodachrome in a coffee can.
Hate-track: Vess beside Pell announcing "full cooperation," the booster flag
painted over in slow news footage. Breather: THE ANTHEM NIGHT — a
minor-league park, the approved harmony hymn starts, one section sings the
old one over it, off-key, unstoppable; the olds in the stands, not singing,
watching the country remember its spine.
**Movement 2 — THE SQUEEZE (48–68).** Pell's warden hour. The random
re-weigh: four crates run heavy — HIS WIN, played straight — and what saves
the plan is the scale tech who frowns, re-runs it, logs "calibration drift,"
and walks away. (The armor is ordinary people declining to see.) The
warehouse raid: a true believer reports it; Pell tosses the place
Norton-style and finds 200 men restoring a carousel for the county fair —
the contraband left on hay trucks the night before, because the net heard
it coming. Hate-track peak: VESS TESTIFIES AGAINST HALE (the Judas beat —
the alternative, known only to her, was a federal receiver). The regime,
suspicious but blind, quietly restores Cricket's pension and drops the tax
lien — kindness as a leash. He cashes nothing.
**Movement 3 — LOAD-OUT AND THE RIDE (68–90).** Send-offs, not goodbyes:
Danny in the barn doorway — "You need a driver?"; Peg packing the kit;
Lita's flag folded into crate five, no dialogue. Loading day: the dockworker
pops crate four on a random check, meets Cricket's eyes, three seconds,
closes the lid, signs ALL CLEAR. Launch night: four heartbeats in four boxes
INTERCUT with the Unity Eve gala — the regime toasting a healed sky while
the thing that ends it rides its own rocket. The coast: knock-code jokes,
the thermos through the vent hatch, SHEN'S PALM FLAT ON CRATE FOUR (held
breath; the film's quietest scene). The offer relayed up the ham chain —
pension, house, flown status, the record amended — and the refusal: "You
can't give back what you never had." Then the burn. "Go for TLI." The one
door physics won't reopen. ACT BREAK.

### ACT 3 — THE ERUPTION (90–120) · the mile taken back
**Landing.** Harmony One touches down; base-station setup; in the bustle,
four shapes slip out and take THE BACKUP ROVER (the backup crew takes the
backup rover — the spare is all they've ever needed). They drive off. WE DO
NOT SEE WHAT THEY DO.
**The empty sites.** The retrieval tour, scheduled to end at Tranquility
(the first flag, the broadcast's crown jewel). Site after site: no flag.
Holes, boot prints, nothing. Earth's conspiracy corner detonates ("THERE
WERE NEVER ANY FLAGS"); Shen starts to say maybe the Americans never— and
Brandt cuts him off: "Don't."
**THE REVEAL.** Their own helmet-cams see it before they do — mission
control and the whole Earth a beat ahead of the astronauts: SIX AMERICAN
FLAGS, vibrant, massed in a ring around the first one — the colors gathered
to one hill — and inside the ring, lawn chairs, a grill, and four old men
holding the most illegal Fourth of July party in human history, streaming
on their own camera. Every empty site answered in one frame. The world
reads the crate lids: OLD IDEAS. OLD CULTURE. OLD CUSTOMS. OLD HABITS.
**The standoff.** Suit-to-suit: told, in plain American, to pack the fuck
off. "Where did you even COME from?" — **"We came in the mail."** Open
loop, planet listening: "Who ARE you?" — **"We're the backup crew. NASA
never processed us out. We're still on the roster, son. This is our
mission."** (Legal, on Earth, goes white: the roster is real; removing them
is removing American officers from their own duty station.) Brandt's orders
say retrieve; he reaches for the nearest flag — and four old men step
together between him and the colors, a color guard forming a wall. Gunny,
open loop: **"You want 'em? Come through us."** Earth erupts — the diner,
the shop, the overpasses — USA, USA — half the smug watch-party caught
chanting before they remember themselves.
**The collapse.** Marwani (on screens) demands Hale end it. Live: "You want
to nationalize me? Go. Fuck. Yourself." Pell storms Frontier for the
override and gets VESS'S REVEAL instead — his own signatures on crate four.
"You wanted the company to cooperate. The company cooperated. The country
didn't." Hale posts one word: **"Look up."** — and the people answer:
porch lights, stadium grids, whole cities flickering in waves across the
night side of the Earth, visible from the Sea of Tranquility. The crew
didn't know it was coming; they get amazed with us. The Accord dies of
ridicule. Brandt accepts a burger for the photograph (the hot mic, earlier,
mid-standoff: "...is that a burger?").
**The Fourth.** Gunny reads Adams' 1776 letter as grace — "...with pomp and
parade... bonfires and illuminations from one end of this continent to the
other" — a 250-year-old prophecy landing on schedule. Tito's three smuggled
shells go up and burn strange and perfect, unslowed by any air. Over the
open loop, to the whole listening planet: **"It's Independence Day."**
Beat. **"God bless America."** WHITE OUT on the third shell.
**Coda.** Public pressure forces the regime to fly the defenders home on
Harmony One — the erasure mission returns carrying the men who beat it,
cramped, eating cold burgers. Three go home. CRICKET STAYS: first Ranger of
Tranquility Base National Historic Park (the preservation statute made the
cookout crew the only legal party on site all along), resupply forced by
the same public pressure. Congress restores the backup crews to the record
— given by law, not offered by a king. Last image: the Ranger waving up at
the night side of Earth — and the Earth, city by city, waving back.

## 8 · CRAFT LAWS IN FORCE (for every later phase)
- Real-person ECHOES (Mamdani-like, Musk-like, Zilis-like), invented names,
  zero biographical claims.
- No narrated relationships; labels are data. Show-don't-tell as mechanics.
- Boomer-craft texture is LOCKED (ham, morse, slide rules, film, cipher,
  knots, CB, Walkman) — the unconfiscatable toolkit.
- Women per the user's law: believable archetypes only — Vess (the
  operator-consort), Peg, Lita. No engineer-heroine inventions.
- Two-Americas cutaway device: same faces, same diner, rising temperature.
- Every scene must turn; the vise tightens in materials; breathers placed
  (anthem night, the thermos, the send-offs).
- Echo-dialogue tell, appended-fact tell, withhold-then-append, rule-of-three
  — banned per standing doctrine; naturalness + blind-attribution gates run
  in Phase 5 with receipts.
