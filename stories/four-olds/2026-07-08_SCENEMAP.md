# THE FOUR OLDS — SCENE MAP
_v1 · 2026-07-08 · Phase 1. Built on the approved BIBLE (canon). 39 scenes.
Tracks: **A** = the olds (Cricket lead) · **B** = Frontier towers (Hale/Vess/
Pell) · **C** = the country (the diner, the shop, two-Americas cuts).
Every scene states want / wall / turn / cost. Corridors were cut or fused._

---

## ACT 1 — THE TAKING (min 0–27)

**1 · COLD OPEN MONTAGE** (C · 0–4)
Election night → the commentator's hope → the inauguration apology → Iran
pallets → Brussels billions → the billionaire tax → one snippet of Hale
grilled → the diner, silent, fireworks ban murmuring on the radio. "Huh."
WANT (regime): the world's applause. WALL: the diner's silence.
TURN: triumph abroad lands as defeat at home — the fault line drawn.
COST: the audience's fury, primed. [PLANT: fireworks ban]

**2 · THE BANK** (A · 4–8)
Cricket, good shirt, asking for an extension on doubled taxes.
WANT: keep the house without begging. WALL: the assessment; the officer's
polite pity. TURN: "You receive… $0.00 quarterly from NASA?" — the ghost
file surfaces as a joke. COST: dignity, spent in a strip-mall office.
[PLANT: ghost file]

**3 · THE BARN & THE NET** (A · 8–12)
The simulator run, flawless and pointless. The Tuesday net: Dutch, Stitch,
Gunny — voices only, razzing through a checklist.
WANT: keep the ritual alive one more week. WALL: it's a mission to nowhere;
even Peg stopped asking. TURN: the logbook wall — every Tuesday since 1972 —
reframes the hobby as a VIGIL. COST: fifty years, measured in shelf-feet.

**4 · DANNY'S CALL / THE ACCORD** (A/C · 12–16)
"Dad. Maybe don't watch the news." The flag plan: retrieved, gifted to the
wronged, Harmony Banner up, broadcast July 4th. Watch-parties cheer;
the shop floor doesn't.
WANT (Danny): soften the blow. WALL: it's everywhere. TURN: Cricket watches
anyway — the vigil now has an enemy. COST: the sky itself politicized.

**5 · THE SEIZURE** (A/B · 16–21)
SUVs at the barn — the trope inverted: they came to TAKE, not to ask.
County lien, Pell's federal tag, the flatbed. They leave the logbooks:
"No salvage value."
WANT: keep the simulator. WALL: the law, politely applied. TURN: contempt
as mercy — the grief hardens into availability. COST: the vigil's altar.
[Pell's first appearance in the flesh]

**6 · THE DINER AFTER** (C · 21–22)
The regulars watch the seizure make the local news. Someone turns the TV off.
WANT: normalcy. WALL: none left. TURN: the first audible "somebody ought to
do something." COST: the country's patience, gone.

**7 · THE MEETING — DOORWAY 1** (A · 22–27)
Legion hall backroom. The net's voices get faces. Mack Boone lays one
photocopied page down: the Class-A spec. "It's a life-support system with a
museum sticker on it. Spec was written by a man in this room." Beat. "So.
You gentlemen want to go to the Moon?" Silence — none of them can say no in
front of the others. Cricket walks out; one breath; returns with an armload
of logbooks: **"I'm current."** The spec sheet goes into the wood stove:
"Then this meeting never happened."
WANT: to be asked, after fifty years. WALL: the yes is a felony; the no is
impossible in company. TURN: the yes as an object. COST: accessories from
tonight; the net goes coded. [PLANT: Mack = the B-track's hidden hand]

## ACT 2A — THE SCHOOL OF OLD MEN (27–48)

**8 · VESS RESTRUCTURES** (B/C · 27–30)
200 jobs cut on live TV; the diner learns her name to curse it. Hale, asked
about the idled equipment, shrugs: "Write it off."
WANT (apparent): appease the Monitorship. WALL: Hale's silence beside her.
TURN: the fired men walk out with their toolboxes — and reconvene at the
dead auction warehouse. COST: her name, spent deliberately.
[HATE-TRACK #1 — double-written]

**9 · THE WAREHOUSE STANDS UP** (A · 30–34)
The kids arrive — Joss off the dock, Tito from the fired shop. Dutch's
binder opens. Six crates, built on the regime's own work order.
WANT: build the Trojan horse in the open. WALL: no payroll, no paper, no
second chances. TURN: Dutch shows the spec's secret — over-built forty
years ago, "the way old engineers over-build everything." COST: every man
in the room, indictable.

**10 · THE 1968 PHYSICAL** (A · 34–38)
Dutch administers the old standard from the binder. Cricket's resting heart
rate embarrasses a smartwatch. Crate endurance drills; old men taking bets.
WANT: prove the bodies still serve. WALL: 79-year-old physiology.
TURN: they pass the only standard that ever mattered — and Dutch quietly
logs a tremor in one hand (dry, unremarked). COST: the knowledge that time,
not Pell, is the enemy with a real shot.

**11 · BOOMER-CRAFT MONTAGE** (A · 38–41)
Coded ham traffic; the Reader's Digest cipher; Kodachrome into the coffee
can; knock-code at the kitchen table until Peg learns it too.
WANT: a network the cloud can't read. WALL: the regime reads everything
else. TURN: the old customs BECOME the operational net. COST: the kids must
learn patience the world untaught them.

**12 · THE SEMINAR** (B/C · 41–43)
A municipal staff training. Pell's deputy, laser pointer, a slide: OLD
IDEAS · OLD CULTURE · OLD CUSTOMS · OLD HABITS — attributed, admiringly, to
its inventor: Mao. Polite applause in an American government building.
WANT (regime): teach the method as best practice. WALL: none — that's the
horror. TURN: the enemy names his own playbook; the title of the film is
put ON SCREEN by the villains. COST: any doubt about what this is.
[THE LAW: commies named]

**13 · THE REPAINT** (B/C · 43–45)
The American flag painted off the booster, in slow news footage; Vess
beside Pell announcing "full cooperation."
WANT (regime): the image cleansed. WALL: the shop floor watching in
silence. TURN: the repaint crew's badges — picked by Mack (unnoticed now,
re-read later). COST: the country's last benefit of the doubt.
[HATE-TRACK #2]

**14 · THE ANTHEM NIGHT** (C · 45–47) — breather
Minor-league park. The approved harmony hymn begins — and one section sings
the old anthem over it, off-key, unstoppable. The olds in the stands, not
singing, watching.
WANT: sing. WALL: ordinance, cameras. TURN: the hymn drowns — the first
public win. COST: fines, names taken. Worth it.

**15 · THE SIM COMES HOME** (A/B · 47–48)
A flatbed returns the simulator, SCRAP in orange across the canopy.
WANT: currency restored. WALL: it should be impossible. TURN: nobody
questions a miracle — but the audience clocks the auction house's name
(Vess's disposal program). COST: a debt to an invisible hand.
[double-written]

## ACT 2B — THE SQUEEZE (48–68)

**16 · THE WEIGH-IN** (A/B · 48–53)
Pell orders a random re-weigh. Four crates run heavy. HE'S RIGHT.
WANT (Pell): the win his instincts promise. WALL: paperwork that's perfect.
TURN: the scale tech frowns, re-runs it, logs "calibration drift," walks
away — Pell's win dissolved by one ordinary refusal. COST: the plan
survives on a stranger's conscience; the margin is now visibly one man
thick. [Pell's win; the armor named]

**17 · VESS TESTIFIES** (B/C · 53–56)
C-SPAN. She corroborates "erratic," endorses the oversight board. The knife
goes in on camera; Hale watches from across the room.
WANT (apparent): her own skin. WALL: his face. TURN: the federal receiver —
which only she knew was on the table — is AVERTED; the public sees only
Judas. COST: the last person America thought was on his side.
[HATE-TRACK #3, peak]

**18 · THE PENSION LEASH** (A · 56–59)
Cricket's pension quietly restored; the tax lien dropped. Unasked.
WANT (regime): a leash disguised as mercy. WALL: Cricket's radar.
TURN: he cashes nothing — frames the check above the workbench like a
specimen. COST: they now know he can't be bought; he now knows they're
watching.

**19 · THE WAREHOUSE RAID** (A/B · 59–63)
A true believer reports it. Pell tosses the warehouse Norton-style — and
finds two hundred men restoring a carousel for the county fair. The
contraband left on hay trucks at midnight; the net heard it coming.
WANT (Pell): the bust. WALL: old customs. TURN: he finds a nothing he KNOWS
was arranged — and says so, quietly, to a carousel horse. COST: Pell stops
being funny. [menace beat]

**20 · SEND-OFFS** (A · 63–66) — breather
Danny in the barn doorway: "You need a driver?" Peg packs the kit like it's
1972. Lita's flag folds into crate five without one line of dialogue.
WANT: leave right. WALL: everything unsaid. TURN: the family doesn't stop
them — it enlists. COST: Peg's face as the truck pulls out. Nobody mourns;
she salutes.

**21 · LOADING DAY — ALL CLEAR** (A · 66–68)
The dock. A loader pops crate four on a random check. Cricket's eyes. Three
seconds. The lid closes. ALL CLEAR, signed.
WANT: four heartbeats onto the manifest. WALL: one honest man's clipboard.
TURN: the honest man chooses — the quiet country picks a side with a
signature. COST: his name on the sheet, forever.

## ACT 2C — THE RIDE (68–90)

**22 · LAUNCH / THE GALA** (A/B/C intercut · 68–74) — set piece
Four heartbeats in four dark boxes; g-force on old faces lit by keychain
lights. Intercut: Unity Day Eve gala, Marwani (screens) toasting "a healed
sky." The kids at the fence line watching the plume climb.
WANT: survive the ascent in a crate. WALL: physics vs. old hearts.
TURN: the thing that ends the regime rides its own rocket. COST: no abort
exists from inside a box.

**23 · THE COAST** (A · 74–79)
Knock-code jokes between crates. The thermos through the vent hatch.
Checklists heard through a bulkhead for three days.
WANT: stay sane, stay hidden. WALL: cramp, dark, age. TURN: the crew
becomes a crew again — fifty years of Tuesdays paying off in taps.
COST: one night Stitch's hands ache too much to answer.

**24 · THE PALM ON THE LID** (A · 79–83) — the quietest scene
Shen walks the cargo bay with the manifest. Stops at crate four. Lays his
palm flat on the lid. Holds.
WANT: pass one man's suspicion. WALL: a political officer's instincts.
TURN: the "vibration dampener" hums its cover story; he moves on.
COST: proof they were never safe.

**25 · THE OFFER** (A · 83–87)
Marwani's voice relayed up the ham chain: flown status, the record amended,
the pension, the house — turn the mission around.
WANT (regime): un-erase one man to erase everything else. WALL: Cricket.
TURN: **"You can't give back what you never had."** COST: the last exit,
closed by choice. [transformation tested]

**26 · TLI — DOORWAY 2** (A · 87–90)
"Go for TLI." The burn physics won't refund.
WANT: the Moon. WALL: none left — that's the point. TURN: the door shuts
itself behind them. COST: everything, gladly. [ACT BREAK]

## ACT 3 — THE ERUPTION (90–120+)

**27 · LANDING** (A · 90–93)
Harmony One down. Base-station bustle. Four shapes slip out; the BACKUP
ROVER rolls — the backup crew takes the backup rover.
WANT: one more sleep unseen. WALL: proximity. TURN: the spare is all
they've ever needed. COST: discovery is now when, not if.

**28 · THE ROVER RUN** (A · 93–94) — THE WITHHOLD
They drive toward the flag sites. WE DO NOT SEE WHAT THEY DO.
TURN: the film openly keeps its secret. COST: the audience's patience,
spent on purpose.

**29 · THE EMPTY SITES** (B/C · 94–98)
The retrieval tour, scheduled to end at Tranquility. Site one: no flag.
Site two: no flag. Earth's conspiracy corner detonates ("THERE WERE NEVER
ANY FLAGS"). Shen: "Perhaps the Americans never actually—" Brandt: "Don't."
WANT (Brandt): follow orders with dignity. WALL: the Moon isn't
cooperating. TURN: the olds created the evidence for the enemy's favorite
lie. COST: the broadcast clock begins to strangle its makers.

**30 · EARTH WATCHES** (C · 98–100)
Diner, shop, watch-parties — everyone confused together for one minute.
TURN: both Americas are watching the SAME thing with the same question,
for the first time. [convergence begins]

**31 · TRANQUILITY — THE REVEAL** (ALL · 100–104) — set piece
The Harmony helmet-cams see it before the astronauts do: SIX VIBRANT FLAGS
massed in a ring around the first one — the colors gathered to one hill —
lawn chairs, a grill, four old men mid-party, their own camera streaming.
On the crate lids, in stencil: OLD IDEAS · OLD CULTURE · OLD CUSTOMS · OLD
HABITS.
TURN: every empty site answered in one frame; theater, planet, and enemy
converge on a single image. COST: hiding, over forever.
[the white-flag lie dies on camera]

**32 · THE STANDOFF** (A · 104–109) — goosebump #1
Suit-to-suit: pack the fuck off. "Where did you even COME from?" — "We came
in the mail." Open loop, planet listening: "Who ARE you?" — "We're the
backup crew. NASA never processed us out. We're still on the roster, son.
This is our mission." Brandt, orders in hand, reaches for the nearest flag —
and four old men step together: a color guard forming a wall. Gunny, open
loop: **"You want 'em? Come through us."**
TURN: Earth erupts — USA, USA — half the smug watch-party caught chanting.
COST: Brandt's career vs. his conscience, live.

**33 · LEGAL GOES WHITE** (B/C · 109–110)
Earth-side lawyers, ashen: the roster is real. Removing them = removing
active-duty American officers from their own duty station.
TURN: the regime's own paperwork mutinies. [ghost file pays]

**34 · HALE DETONATES** (B/C · 110–112)
The demand comes live. Hale glances off-camera — Vess in the wings, the
youngest on her hip. One slow nod. **"You want to nationalize me? Go. Fuck.
Yourself."**
TURN: the sellout re-read in one sentence; the laughter goes planetary.
COST: the empire he built, offered up. [C-cut: stock craters, then soars]

**35 · VESS'S SAFE MOMENT** (B · 112–114)
Pell storms Frontier with marshals. She lays a folder on the table — his
own signatures. "You inspected crate four yourself, Administrator. I
scheduled you. Twice." Beat. "You wanted the company to cooperate. The
company cooperated." Walks to the window. "The country didn't."
TURN: the hated woman was the mastermind; boos flip to cheers; Mack, the
firings, the auction, the schedule — all re-read in five seconds.
COST: her cover, spent exactly on schedule. [the Norton safe]

**36 · "LOOK UP."** (ALL · 114–117)
One post on the platform he still owns. Porch lights. Stadium grids. Whole
cities flickering in waves across the night side of the Earth — seen from
the Sea of Tranquility. The crew didn't know it was coming.
TURN: the country answers as one organism; the olds get amazed WITH us.

**37 · THE FOURTH** (A · 117–119) — goosebump #2
Gunny reads Adams' 1776 letter as grace: "…with pomp and parade… bonfires
and illuminations from one end of this continent to the other." Burgers for
the Harmony crew (the hot mic — "…is that a burger?" — paid). Tito's three
smuggled shells rise and burn strange and perfect, unslowed by any air.
Over the open loop, to the whole listening planet: **"It's Independence
Day."** Beat. **"God bless America."** WHITE OUT on the third shell.
[PLANTS PAY: fireworks ban · Adams · the grill · the 51st flag flying]

**38 · CODA — THE RIDE HOME** (A/B · 119–121)
Three olds cramped aboard Harmony One, eating cold burgers. Brandt keeps
the photograph. Shen stares at the bulkhead. Gentle comedy; total silence
about it in the press kit.
TURN: the erasure mission returns carrying the men who beat it.

**39 · CODA — THE RANGER** (A · 121–123)
Cricket stays: first Ranger of Tranquility Base National Historic Park —
the preservation statute made the cookout crew the only legal party on
site all along. Resupply forced by the same public pressure. Congress
restores the backup crews to the record — given by law, not offered by a
king. FINAL IMAGE: the Ranger waves up at the night side of Earth — and
the Earth, city by city, waves back. [END]
