# THE FOUR OLDS — recruitment redesign (structure before scenes)

User-directed (CP3 loop). Replaces the dark-ask recruitment with a stated
legitimate purpose + the hmm-chain + the decoy riders. No scenes rewritten
until this design is approved.

## 1 · The cover (stated, true as far as it goes)
The Accord needs six museum-grade cases to bring six flags home. Frontier's
certified fabrication is gone — Vess closed Bay Two (sc08 now
double-load-bearing: she CREATED the vacancy). Budget line + "cases aren't
human-rated hardware, they're boxes — no badges required" → the logistics
contractor subcontracts to "heritage craftsmen": cheap old experts.
THE INSULT IS THE RECRUITER: "it's just flags," "old-timers still know how
to build things." The regime's contempt brings the men in — rage fuel on
the page, and the regime pays for the instrument of its own humiliation.

## 1b · Why patriots take the job (the custodial yes)
They would never help take the flags down — but making sure the flags come
down RIGHT is the flag code itself: colors are retired with honors, by
hands that know how. The yes, spoken at the hall (Gunny's to make): "If
they're coming down regardless — and they are — then they come down right.
Folded right, cased right, carried right. Not by the lowest bidder." The
regime thinks it hired cheap labor; the men are mounting an honor guard
over their own flags' caskets. The step-in at the midpoint becomes
inevitable, not convenient: an honor guard escorts the colors.
- The audience's theory shop stays open (swap? sabotage? steal them back
  on Earth before the gifting?) — none confirmed; the midpoint beats them
  all.
- THE SOCIAL COST (new, load-bearing): the town hears the olds are
  building the boxes and reads it as collaboration. The diner goes cold —
  Buck won't look at Cricket; the coffee comes slower; nobody says the
  word. The men take it in silence (they can't explain). Heart-ledger runs
  negative through the middle; at the reveal, Buck's "There they are."
  becomes an apology. The reveal redeems them in their town's eyes, not
  just against the regime.

## Marwani line change (applied, sc04)
"Flags of surrender, if we are honest" → "Some call those flags of
surrender. I call them flags of peace. Let this be our gesture to the rest
of the world: we come in peace." — the regime perverting the Apollo 11
plaque; rhymes at the standoff against "We came in the mail."

## 2 · Who knows what, when
- MACK: knows four of six cases are configured for men. States nothing.
  Runs the job straight. (Whether he hoped or planned stays unanswerable —
  he built doors, not destinies.)
- THE OLDS: take a paying job, swallowing the insult; each keeps his
  private feelings about the flags to himself. DUTCH owns the hmm-chain.
- THE KIDS: hired hands. Joss floats the audience's wrong guess ("we're
  switching the crates, right?") — voiced once, never confirmed.
- THE AUDIENCE: ahead of the olds (they saw "Net's open. Wednesday." and
  Mack's notebook), behind Mack. The engaged seat.
- PEG: reads number groups aloud at the kitchen table, asks nothing.
- VESS: above everything, invisible until the safe.

## 3 · The hmm-chain (Dutch's; each raised once, then self-dismissed)
1. The spec is overkill — "you could put a man in this." (Laughed off:
   government money.)
2. Only four of six cases get the padded interior. ("Different artifacts,
   different cradles.")
3. The confidentiality rules feel heavier than trade secrecy.
4. 1968 flight physicals "for the insurance." ("Standards are standards.")
5. THE FOUR VISITORS — after this one he stops dismissing and runs the
   math at midnight.

## 4 · The four visitors (the decoy riders)
Four fit men in company windbreakers walk the shop with Mack, get
measured, climb into the cases one by one, say nothing, leave. The
audience's coffee-cup question ("someone's riding") gets a standing wrong
answer. Why they evaporate: THE EXISTING LAW — they're employees; the
Monitorship logs every badge; one payroll fingerprint = nationalization
and prison. The young men legally exist, so they can't go. Four old ghosts
can. The theme does the plotting; nobody explains it on camera.

## 5 · The midpoint (unchanged in function, richer in meaning)
Dutch's scrubber math → the couch → "Who's riding, Boone?" → "So. You
gentlemen want to go to the Moon?" → the olds STEP IN and claim the
couches nobody assigned them (Cricket unlaces his boots; wordless).
Volunteers, not chosen. "I'm current." stays at the hall as craft
credentials — the midpoint re-reads it silently; it is not respoken.

## 6 · The numbers (all load-bearing)
6 flags (history) → 6 cases (the regime's math) → 4 + 2 (the secret math):
four men, two of "Gunny's gear" (the cookout withhold). FOUR comes from
Mao (the seminar slide, the stencils, "four obsolete men") — never from
the flag count. The Mao stencils now get painted POST-midpoint, chosen by
the men themselves: the regime taught the categories; the men claim them.
