ACTION: A desk lamp burns over a plywood table. Mack Boone, 55, freight-and-salvage, sits alone with a freight booking sheet squared in front of him and a floor scale dragged up against his boot.
ACTION: He lifts an archival box onto the scale. The board is empty, flaps hanging open.
ACTION: The needle swings up and settles. He watches it, then pencils the number onto the sheet.
ACTION: He sets the box off and lifts the next one. Same swing. Same heavy number.
ACTION: Four numbers run down the sheet now, each circled hard enough to dent the paper.
ACTION: He runs the pencil down the shipping weight printed on the manifest and holds it beside his own column. Each of his four numbers sits thirty kilos above the printed weight.
ACTION: He circles the gap and sets the pencil down. He sits there a while.
ACTION: He stands. He walks the dark shop floor past the welding bays and the crates stacked up into the rafters.
ACTION: He stops at a low shape under a tarp by Bay Two. He takes a corner of the canvas and lifts it.
ACTION: Under it sits a couch. Strapped down to the cushions, a packed form the size and shape of a MAN.
ACTION: He looks at it a long moment. He lets the tarp fall and carries the sheet back to the table where the others sit in the lamp light.
MACK: Nothing on this manifest breathes, gentlemen.
ACTION: Earl "Cricket" Dawes, 79, sets his coffee down on the plywood and answers without getting up.
CRICKET: Tuesday the truck carries six boxes out on your paper. Six of us are in them. The load reads right because that dummy already weighs what a man weighs. They wave it through the gate and nobody counts us at the yard.
ACTION: Mack stays on his feet. He looks down at Cricket.
MACK: You understand there's no backing out once you're up there. Bad heart at four g's, your age, no doctor closer than a quarter million miles.
ACTION: Gunny Tibbs, 71, Navy retired, leans back against the bench and looks up at him. Not unkindly.
GUNNY: Boone, I've buried better men than me for worse reasons than this. Sit down or go home. Either's fine. Quit standing there doing arithmetic on our behalf.
ACTION: Joss Reed, 26, dock hand, watches from the dark past the last crate.
JOSS: You're all insane. What do you need from me?
ACTION: Tito Alvarez, 24, the welder, brings his knuckles twice against the crate wall.
ACTION: Two knocks on plywood carry across the shop.
ACTION: Nobody at the table says anything.
ACTION: Mack pulls the bench out and sits down.
ACTION: He reaches into his coat and takes out his pen.
