@@SCENE@@
ACTION: A wood stove ticks in the corner of the Legion back room, and a row of folding chairs stands squared up against the paneling.
ACTION: Unit photographs hang along the wall in cheap frames, the oldest of them back to Korea.
ACTION: MACK Boone, 55, freight hauler, sets a thin folder on the card table and slides one sheet halfway out of it.
ACTION: Cricket, Gunny, Stitch, and Dutch sit around the table with coffee going cold in front of them.
MACK: Read that before I say a word. Then you can throw me out if you want.
ACTION: Mack pushes the top sheet to the middle of the table.
ACTION: ON THE MANIFEST -- LUNAR HERITAGE NEUTRALIZATION ACCORD, ARTIFACT RETRIEVAL. SIX (6) CONTAINMENT UNITS, CLASS-A ARCHIVAL. OUTBOUND CONFIGURATION, EMPTY.
ACTION: Dutch picks the manifest up and reads the whole of it twice before he puts it down.
DUTCH: Six boxes. They go up empty.
MACK: And they come back down with the flags in them.
ACTION: Nobody says anything, and the stove ticks.
ACTION: Mack turns the sheet over to a printed email and reads it off with no color in his voice at all.
MACK: Containment units are non-crew hardware. No certification pathway required. Recommend sourcing retired fabrication and aerospace personnel at heritage-labor rates.
ACTION: He sets the page down where they can all see it.
MACK: Heritage-labor rates. That's you, gentlemen. That's this whole room.
ACTION: Stitch turns his cup a quarter turn on the table and looks at the email.
STITCH: Cheap old men. To box up the—
ACTION: He lets it drop, and not one man at the table picks it up for him.
ACTION: Dutch has the spec page out of his own packet already and runs a thumb down the margin.
DUTCH: This is my standard. The '98 archival spec. Word for word.
MACK: Nobody's improved it.
ACTION: Gunny stands, squares his chair back against the wall, and stays on his feet.
GUNNY: Somebody's going to fold those colors into them, on live television, with the whole world grading the job. I'd sooner it be men who know how a flag is handled than whoever else answers that email. We're the whoever-else, boys.
ACTION: A short, hard laugh goes around the table and dies out fast.
ACTION: Mack deals a stapled packet to each man like he's dealing cards, and only Dutch reads all of his.
ACTION: Mack taps the last page with one finger.
MACK: No phones in the shop, no pictures. And whatever gets built in there, it stays inside those walls.
ACTION: Stitch signs his without turning past the first page.
STITCH: Confidential flag boxes.
MACK: That's the job.
ACTION: Gunny signs, and Dutch signs under him, slow, down at the bottom of the last page.
ACTION: Cricket slides his own packet back to the middle with no mark on it, puts his cap on, and gets up.
ACTION: The back door BANGS behind him.
ACTION: Nobody moves, and Gunny pours a cup off the percolator he doesn't drink.
ACTION: A minute later the door opens and Cricket comes back in with the cap still on his head.
ACTION: He takes his packet up off the table, turns to the spec page, and reads it standing where he is.
ACTION: He picks up the pen and signs last, at the bottom, under Dutch.
CRICKET: When's Monday.
