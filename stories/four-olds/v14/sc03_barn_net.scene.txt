@@SCENE@@
ACTION: A hand pulls a cord in the dark and the pull-switch clicks.
ACTION: A work light swings and steadies over the APOLLO PROCEDURES TRAINER, hand-machined gauges set in a plywood panel, the numbers inked by hand.
ACTION: Two seats ride in a welded steel cradle bolted to the barn floor. The couch is an old truck bench, patched at the seams.
ACTION: A triangular window is cut in the panel. Behind it, painted on the barn wall, hangs a gray Moon the size of a wagon wheel.
ACTION: ALONG THE BACK WALL -- spiral notebooks stand floor to roof, hundreds of them, every spine dated in the same block hand. The bottom shelf starts in 1972.
ACTION: CRICKET -- Earl Dawes, 79, seed-cap, a farmer's forearms, settles into the left seat and squares a clipboard on his knee.
ACTION: He thumbs a switch. A speaker on the shelf pops and hisses.
ACTION: A relay behind the panel BUZZES, a steady low note.
CRICKET: Net's open. Tuesday, nineteen-oh-two local.
ACTION: The speaker crackles. A dry voice comes up out of it.
GUNNY (RADIO): Copy nineteen-oh-two. Hold for the count.
ACTION: Cricket clicks a stopwatch and sets it face-up on the panel.
GUNNY (RADIO): Apollo Eighteen. Lunar descent rehearsal. Number two thousand, eight hundred and six. Sound off.
CRICKET: Commander, up.
DUTCH (RADIO): Systems, up. I want the log to show I said the Bus B relay is due. Been saying it.
GUNNY (RADIO): Noted for the log, Dutch. Same as last week.
ACTION: A third speaker, a smaller one clipped to the cradle, opens with a slow Texas drawl already in progress.
STITCH (RADIO): Backup's up, and listen, before we go anyplace. You boys hear they sold off Ferris Kettle's whole place Saturday.
GUNNY (RADIO): Stitch. Keep the net clean.
STITCH (RADIO): I'm keepin' it clean, I'm just tellin' you. Forty-some years he farms that ground and it goes for near nothin' on a Saturday. Fella from two counties over bought the good disc for a hundred dollars.
ACTION: Cricket runs a pencil down the clipboard without looking up.
CRICKET: Program's up. Coming to ignition.
DUTCH (RADIO): Confirm attitude before you light it. I don't want it off a hair like October.
STITCH (RADIO): A hundred dollars. Man stood right next to me and never blinked.
GUNNY (RADIO): We are hot for the burn. Ferris Kettle's disc can wait.
STITCH (RADIO): It has waited. It'll keep waitin'. That's the thing about it.
ACTION: The buzz holds steady behind the panel. A needle marked ALT swings and settles.
CRICKET: Ignition. Throttle up.
ACTION: Cricket eases a lever forward in the cradle. The whole seat trembles on its springs.
DUTCH (RADIO): Descent rate looks nominal. I've got it logged nominal.
STITCH (RADIO): Anyway his boy didn't want none of it. Boy's up in Omaha sellin' insurance. Sells insurance to farmers, that's the part that gets me.
GUNNY (RADIO): We're at high gate. Somebody fly the airplane.
CRICKET: Forty thousand feet. Coming down.
ACTION: Dutch's voice sharpens, thin over the speaker.
DUTCH (RADIO): Hold it. My inverter's reading soft. Cricket, is your Bus B needle steady on your side.
ACTION: Cricket leans to the panel. The needle marked BUS B sits still, then jumps a hair and falls back.
CRICKET: Steady. Wait.
ACTION: The needle wanders down, slow, off the mark. The steady buzz roughens and starts to stutter.
DUTCH (RADIO): Steady or does it stutter. Tell me which.
CRICKET: Stutters.
DUTCH (RADIO): That's the relay. Forty-year-old relay, and I'm allowed to say it, I put it on the log in nineteen eighty-six. Kick it. Low left corner of the panel.
ACTION: Cricket makes a fist and raps the plywood, low and left, once.
ACTION: The buzz catches, smooths out. The BUS B needle climbs back and settles on the mark.
STITCH (RADIO): So I says to his boy, that disc'll outlast your whole insurance company, and he laughs at me.
CRICKET: Three thousand feet. Rate's good.
GUNNY (RADIO): Copy three thousand.
ACTION: Nobody says a word about the relay. Dutch clears his throat and lets it sit.
DUTCH (RADIO): Log shows Bus B corrected.
STITCH (RADIO): Y'all want to know what I think, fifty years of Tuesdays, the Moon ain't gonna sneak up on us tonight. It's right where it was.
GUNNY (RADIO): Keep the net clean, Stitch. Last time.
STITCH (RADIO): I'll keep it clean when there's somethin' worth cleanin'. Man bought a two-hundred-dollar disc for a hundred and I am gonna sit with that a minute.
ACTION: Cricket flies on through it, eyes on the gauges, thumb tapping the lever.
CRICKET: Twelve hundred feet. Pitching over.
DUTCH (RADIO): Fuel's a little heavy on my board. You've got margin.
CRICKET: Copy margin. Seven hundred feet.
STITCH (RADIO): His wife made a casserole for the auction crowd and it was the good kind, the one with the crackers on top.
CRICKET: Five hundred feet.
ACTION: The stutter of talk over the speakers thins out. Stitch's voice trails and stops.
CRICKET: Three hundred feet.
ACTION: The barn goes quiet. Nobody calls for quiet. The buzz runs on alone under the work light.
CRICKET: Two hundred. Coming down slow.
ACTION: Cricket eases the lever back. The cradle groans on its springs and holds.
CRICKET: Forty feet. Kicking up a little.
ACTION: A green bulb on the panel snaps on.
CRICKET: Contact light. Engine stop.
ACTION: Cricket lets go of the lever. His hand rests flat on his knee.
ACTION: The buzz cuts out. The barn holds still.
STITCH (RADIO): You were heavy on that throttle at the end there, Colonel.
CRICKET: I was.
GUNNY (RADIO): The man is on the ground, Colonel. Leave him be.
ACTION: Dutch's dry voice comes up last.
DUTCH (RADIO): Relay held.
CRICKET: All right.
ACTION: Cricket picks up the stopwatch and clicks it dead.
GUNNY (RADIO): Net closed. Same time Tuesday.
STITCH (RADIO): God willing.
ACTION: Two clicks come over the speaker, sharp and close.
ACTION: Cricket keys his mike twice. Two clicks answer from Dutch. Two from Stitch.
ACTION: The speakers hiss, then die when Cricket thumbs the switch.
ACTION: He opens the top notebook to a fresh page and writes it out in ballpoint block letters, the entry number, then the words bus B corrected, then contact at nineteen forty-one, then crew present by radio.
ACTION: He stands and runs his thumb down the dated spines along the back wall, from 1972 up to tonight.
ACTION: AT THE DOOR -- Cricket hangs a padlock through the hasp and pulls it once. It holds.
ACTION: He reaches up and the work light dies under his hand.
ACTION: Outside, Cricket crosses the dark yard toward the farmhouse. Every window in it is dark.
