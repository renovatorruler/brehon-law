# THE FOUR OLDS — the audience model (2026-07-07)
_The knowledge architecture: what the audience knows, when, and what is
withheld. Rule zero (user): NO CHEATING — no magical appearances; every
mechanic shown honestly. The amaze comes from withheld MEANING, never from
withheld MECHANICS._

## THE ONE BIG WITHHOLD: THE COOKOUT
The audience knows, almost from the poster, that six Americans will be at
Tranquility defending the flags. What they must NOT know is HOW. The film
lets everyone — audience, regime, world — assume the obvious model: a
standoff. Grim men guarding poles. Maybe weapons. The cookout detonates that
assumption in one shot.

**How to withhold it honestly (caper grammar):**
1. **Misread props.** Show ALL the cargo being loaded — honestly — but let
   its purpose be misread: crates (read as equipment), Gunny's sacred
   personal-allowance box (read as flag-ceremony gear), the folded umbrella
   (never unfolded on screen), Tito's grandmother's flag (read as a
   REPLACEMENT flag — feeding the wrong model that they'll re-plant). Every
   item is on screen in Act 2. Nothing is understood.
2. **The cutaway.** One crew argument in the coast phase: "And when Harmony
   lands? We can't fight them." Cricket: "We're not going to fight them."
   Beat. "Gunny, show 'em." — CUT TO EARTH. The classic caper cut: the plan
   exists, the audience knows it exists, and the film visibly declines to
   share it. That's a promise, not a cheat.
3. **The coverage gap, structurally motivated.** We ride WITH the Bell the
   whole way — build, launch, coast, landing burn, touchdown, engines off,
   cheer — and then the film LEAVES the Moon for the Earth-bedlam sequence
   (which the story needs anyway: the manhunt, Hale's squeeze, the Harmony
   launch). We never see the crew unpack. When we return to Tranquility, it
   is ONLY through the Harmony lander's descent camera — and the world, the
   Harmony crew, and the theater discover the cookout in the same frame:
   lawn chairs, umbrella, grill smoke-shimmer, rover donut tracks, an old
   man waving a spatula. The in-film audience of billions and the real
   audience become one audience at the reveal. That convergence IS the
   set piece.
4. **Production note:** the cookout must be protected like a twist — not in
   the trailer. The trailer sells the theft, the launch, and the standoff
   expectation. The lawn chairs stay secret.

## THREAD-BY-THREAD KNOWLEDGE DESIGN

**HALE (dramatic irony, with a wink):** the audience is let IN on the
boneyard gate and the mailed keys — not with a statement, with a wink (his
assistant asks about the pylon ship's insurance; Hale, deadpan: "Write it
off."). For two acts the audience savors watching the world call him a
sellout WHILE KNOWING. The fuck-you is not a reveal — it's a release. (The
alternative — hiding his hand until Act 3 — buys one gasp and costs two
acts of the best irony in the film. Rejected.)

**THE LIGHTS (the user's fusion, adopted):** Hale cannot make the Earth
flicker. He can make it WANT to. After the fuck-you goes planetary, he posts
one word on the platform he still owns: **"Look up."** — and the people do
the rest: porch lights, stadium lights, city grids, flashing in waves across
the night side. The character prompts; the PEOPLE deliver (the greatness law
demands the people, not one man). From Tranquility: the planet applauding in
photons. Nobody on the crew knows it's coming — the crew gets amazed too;
the one moment the withholding runs AGAINST our heroes, tenderly.

**THE DESTRUCT BUTTON (suspense, planted early):** the audience learns in
Act 2A — during dark-launch planning, stated as dry procedure — that any
launch from US soil flies with a range-safety destruct package, and the
regime holds the key. So the launch plays under Hitchcock's bomb: the
audience watches the climb KNOWING the button exists. The refusal — the
range officer standing up and walking out — pays the plant. (Cascade-of-no
mechanism adopted: the regime dies bolt by bolt, each refusal a face.)

**THE WHITE FLAGS (known fact, withheld meaning):** planted in Act 1 by the
REGIME itself — the propaganda reel: "Fifty years of radiation have bleached
them white. White flags. Flags of surrender. Let us retire them with
dignity." The audience carries that framing to the Moon — and the crew's
reframe at the climax flips the same fact: fifty years of pure sunlight, no
shade, no rest — still standing. Down there white means surrender. Up here
it means tested. Same data, new meaning: the thesis performed as a reveal.

**THE GHOST FILE (plant as joke, payoff as law):** Act 1, the barn: among
Cricket's mail, a government envelope addressed to "DAWES, E. — ASTRONAUT
(ACTIVE)" — a $0.00 pay stub he's apparently received quarterly for fifty
years. Played for one laugh ("they never could finish anything"). Forgotten.
Then at Tranquility, when the Harmony commander needs "custody transfer from
a representative of the owning nation," Cricket produces the stub: the
ranking — the ONLY — active US astronaut on site. DENIED. The erasure
missed one file, and the file wins. (Audience should be ~5 seconds ahead of
the reveal — the best kind: they remember the envelope as he reaches for it.)

**MARWANI (transparency, no mystery):** the villain is fully legible from
Act 1 — including the soft-side beat (moved by the Apollo footage, crates it
BECAUSE it's powerful). No twist, no hidden motive. Tyranny-by-consensus is
scariest in plain daylight; all the film's mystery budget is spent on our
side of the board.

## THE KNOWLEDGE GRID (coarse)
| Beat | Audience | The world (in-film) | The regime |
|---|---|---|---|
| Boneyard build | knows all | knows nothing | suspects scrap theft |
| Dark launch | knows + destruct dread | learns at +11 min | knows, orders destruct, refused |
| Coast/temptation | knows all but THE PLAN | propaganda version | tracking, bargaining |
| Landing → gap | mechanics shown, setup unseen | "they're up there" | assumes standoff |
| THE REVEAL | discovers WITH the world | discovers | discovers |
| Ghost file | 5 seconds ahead | live on air | blindsided by own paperwork |
| The lights | surprised WITH the crew | is the author of it | irrelevant now |

## WHY THIS SHAPE
The film's subject is broadcasting (a regime staging a global telecast; a
crew hijacking it). So the knowledge design converges every audience — the
theater, the world, the enemy — onto single shared frames at the moments
that matter: the cookout reveal, the DENIED, the lights. A movie about
seizing the world's screen should climax with everyone watching the same
image at the same time. Form matches theme; that's the forest.
