@@SCENE@@
ACTION: Wind works at the barn boards. Under it the flight trainer holds one low electrical hum, and the percolator knocks uneven on the hot plate.
ACTION: Out on the gravel a truck comes up the drive, slows, and shuts off. A door opens and claps to. Boots cross the yard.
GUNNY: (approaching) Earl. It's froze solid out there.
CRICKET: Gunny.
GUNNY: Coffee's already going.
ACTION: A tin cup sets down on the workbench.
GUNNY: You slept out here again.
CRICKET: Percolator's ahead of you. Get yourself a cup.
GUNNY: I asked did you sleep out here.
CRICKET: There's a chair by the trainer.
ACTION: Two more trucks come up the gravel, one behind the other, and cut off. Doors, and two sets of boots on the boards.
STITCH: (approaching) That road past the culvert is a sheet of ice, I come up on it near sideways. Evening, Earl. Gunny.
DUTCH: My defroster quit back in November. I drove the whole way with the window cracked.
CRICKET: Dutch. Stitch. Coffee's on.
GUNNY: All right. Barn's secured, coffee's poured. We'll open her up.
GUNNY: Apollo Eighteen. Lunar descent rehearsal. Number two thousand, eight hundred and six.
CRICKET: Here.
DUTCH: Present.
STITCH: Accounted for.
STITCH: I stayed up Sunday night and watched that landing again. The whole business. That boy they run the parade for down in Houston.
STITCH: Computer flew it, Gunny. Flew the approach, found its own spot, and set it down, and he just had his hands in his lap the whole way.
GUNNY: If you're bringing a case you'll bring it in order. Cricket's the bench, same as he always is.
STITCH: That's what I'm doing. I'm prosecuting. Cricket, I want a finding out of you tonight.
CRICKET: Go ahead.
ACTION: A ledger page turns.
DUTCH: (reading) He had it in hand from four hundred feet. October the ninth. Says so right there where I wrote it down.
STITCH: Four hundred feet. All right, four hundred feet of flying on a landing the box already had finished for him.
DUTCH: Three hundred and eighty. I took it off the loop myself, so we'll use the real one.
STITCH: See now, that there is about the last little sliver of it, and they hang a medal on the man and march him down the street.
GUNNY: One man at a time. We hear the figure, then we hear the argument. There's an order to a proceeding, McGrew.
STITCH: I've heard the figure. In my court a computer landing doesn't earn a fella a parade. Call that a rule of evidence.
DUTCH: There isn't any such rule.
STITCH: There is now. Entered it just this second.
GUNNY: You can't go entering rules in the middle of your own prosecution.
STITCH: I'm the prosecutor and the historian both, Gunny, so I surely can.
ACTION: A cup knocks down on the workbench.
STITCH: Cricket. Does hand-flying from three hundred and eighty count for the man or don't it.
CRICKET: Manual's manual. Count it.
STITCH: The bench counts it, and I'll tell you it still doesn't come to a whole lot.
STITCH: Here's my finding. Not a one of these kids they're decorating would've got past the flight surgeon in our day, and you all know that as well as I do.
DUTCH: That part's true. On the medical alone they'd have washed every one of them out.
GUNNY: Is that the finding of this crew, then.
DUTCH: Concur.
GUNNY: Dawes.
ACTION: The percolator knocks twice on the hot plate.
GUNNY: Let the record show he concurs.
GUNNY: All right. Cups in the sink. Mind the cord on the trainer going out.
GUNNY: Net closed. Same time Tuesday.
CRICKET: God willing.
ACTION: Chairs scrape back and boots cross to the door.
ACTION: Out in the yard three truck doors clap shut, not together.
ACTION: One engine turns over and rolls down the gravel. After a while a second pulls out behind it.
ACTION: The third truck starts and goes, and the gravel goes quiet.
ACTION: A padlock hasp pulls closed with one pull.
ACTION: Wind runs along the barn boards. Off across the yard a screen door bangs once and settles.
