# DEEPER — Character Bible (the iceberg)

*Working title.* A dark comedy. A university janitor, certain he is a misunderstood
genius, mistakes the sewage backing up in the basement for the thread of a vast
cover-up, and the more nothing he finds the deeper he is sure it goes.

Reference, not script. **Surface** = shown, never told. **Submerged** = informs only.
**Deep** = no possible use in the film, kept anyway, because real people are mostly
made of things that never come up. Most of this is Deep. Each person gets a natal
chart as a temperament anchor, a wheel to check them against so they never drift.

**The buried engine of the whole film.** Everyone here is a person whose life did not
become what their younger self was promised. Each has made a private peace with that,
in a different key. Gary is the only one who refused, and in place of that peace he
built a conspiracy, a story in which his ordinary life is a crime committed against
him rather than a fact about him. The dark thing is not in the basement. It is the
ordinary grief of being ordinary, which every person in this film carries and only
Gary will not set down. The comedy and the ache both come from there.

---

## THE FORMAL QUESTIONS — the material life and the essence

What do they do for money, and is it enough? What is the pressure on it now? What is
the one engine under all the behavior, embodied, never named?

**GARY SOBEK.** A custodian's wage, nine years on the night crew, and as of this week
nothing at all; the savings are weeks from gone and the rent is not. — *Pressure:*
fired, broke, the real life (Sal's job offer, Donna) sliding past, and a deadline he
invented, the cover-up "closing in." — *Essence:* a man who would rather be persecuted
than ordinary. The conspiracy is the only story in which his small life is the result
of his importance and not his limits. The one question that empties him out is *what
if you are just a guy.*

**SAL DiPIETRO.** Union foreman, a house half paid off, a wife, two kids, steady. —
*Pressure:* he is watching his oldest friend go under and can't reach him, and he
keeps holding out the Whitmore job, which is also just Sal needing Gary near. —
*Essence:* loyalty as a form of grief. He decided forty years ago that loving Gary
meant letting Gary be the smart one, and he will pay that bill to the end.

**ROYCE.** A garden unit, a 2 a.m. AM radio slot that pays in dignity, a tip jar, the
kindness of the lonely. — *Pressure:* the audience is literally dying off, and he
needs a believer now more than they need him. — *Essence:* a man who lost everything
for being wrong and could only go on by deciding he was right. Being believed in is
the last warm thing he owns. He is Gary's future in a good shiny blazer.

**DONNA.** Twelve years of diner wages and tips, a small careful apartment. —
*Pressure:* she is forty-six and tired of waiting on a man who cannot see her. —
*Essence:* she sees the decent ordinary man inside Gary and would take him gladly, and
she is invisible to him because she is not part of the myth. The real visa to the
normal country, held out, unread.

**RAY HALLORAN.** Facilities director, a mortgage, two in college, a pension he counts
toward like rosary beads. — *Pressure:* he carried Gary for nine years, finally
couldn't, and it has now, insanely, gotten him chained in a basement. — *Essence:* the
decent middle manager worn smooth by carrying other men's problems; a disappointed man
who made his peace, which is the exact peace Gary could not make. He is what Gary could
have *survived* becoming.

**PROFESSOR LIND.** Tenured, comfortable, a sabbatical cottage. — *Pressure:* none he
would name; the soft late pressure of a man who suspects he was never quite
first-rate. — *Essence:* a kind, tired teacher who wipes nonsense off a board without
once dreaming a whole man hangs on it. Settled, and at peace with settling.

## GARY SOBEK — the man who would rather be persecuted than ordinary

**Natal chart.** Born 4 February 1978, a little after noon. **Aquarius sun conjunct
Mercury** — the self welded to the intellect, the outsider certain he sees the system
the insiders cannot. **Saturn in Taurus square the sun** — a boot on the neck he has
always located outside himself. **Moon in Pisces, trine Neptune** — he lives a
half-inch off the ground, in the better story. The whole man in a line: a mind that
mistook being unusual for being right.

**The verdict.** At nine, a county test put him in the top one percent, and a teacher
named Mrs. Vorhees told his mother, in front of him, that Gary was the kind of mind
that comes along rarely. He has lived off that one sentence for thirty-nine years the
way a town lives off a mine that closed.

**Childhood & the parts that go nowhere.** Advanced track, then a scholarship to a
school across the county where, for the first time, he was not the smartest in the
room, and rather than be second he became truant. His father, a courthouse custodian
for thirty years, told him a man's work is a man's work and was, privately, enormous
with pride about the test, and died before Gary could become anything that would prove
it — so the verdict was never revised, and now never can be. There is a manuscript,
begun at twenty-six, a unified theory of "systems," three hundred and forty pages, that
one vanity press declined and that lives in a drawer and is referred to, when at all,
as *in progress.*

**The body.** Unremarkable on purpose, the name stitched over the pocket, careful
hands, the too-good posture of a man performing a dignity no one is contesting.

**Tastes, habits, superstitions.** Brings his own chalk from home, yellow, because the
school's is "compromised." Does the Sunday acrostic in pen in fourteen minutes and
leaves it on the table for a roommate he has not had since 2003. Cannot abide a
fraction left un-reduced. Eats standing at the counter so as not to lose the thread.

**People who never appear.** *Mrs. Vorhees,* the teacher, long dead, who said the
sentence. *His father,* dead, whose pride he is still trying to earn. *His mother,* in
a memory unit, who tells the aides her son is "at the university" and lets them assume
he teaches.

**Things he can do that never matter.** He really can read a building's blueprints
cold — the one true gift, the thing Sal means when he says *you see things.* He can fix
any machine. He is, in plain fact, somewhat smart. That is the cruelty of the whole
thing. Not a genius. Just bright enough to build a cathedral out of being overlooked.

**The plot core.**
- *(Surface)* the grandiosity, the signature, the certainty, the small sad knowing nod.
- *(Submerged)* if the conspiracy is false, he is a man who threw his life away over a
  sentence a teacher said when he was nine. That is the one thing he cannot survive
  knowing, so the delusion is load-bearing. He is not chasing the truth. He is running
  from it.
- *(Deep)* under the place under the place, he knows the manuscript was not good. He has
  known since the day it came back. Everything since has been built over that grave so
  he never has to stand on it.

## SAL DiPIETRO — loyalty as grief

**Natal chart.** 9 May 1977. **Taurus sun** (the fixed, patient ground), **Cancer moon**
(loyalty as a body function), **Capricorn rising** (the foreman's spine). Saturn trine
the sun: built to carry weight without complaint.

**The thirty-year contract.** He and Gary were boys together. Sal was "the dumb one,"
Gary "the smart one," and Sal made a life while Gary made a theory, and Sal has never
once resented it, because being the dumb one cost him nothing and being the smart one
was the only thing Gary had. *(Submerged:* Sal has known for thirty years that Gary is
not what Gary thinks, and loved him anyway, and never said it, and that silence is its
own long sorrow.* )* There is a real debt under the love: at seventeen Gary took a
charge that was Sal's, did the community service, never mentioned it again. Sal is
still paying that back, and always will be.

**The thing that goes nowhere.** Sal genuinely cannot read well; Gary used to walk him
through the prints, patient, never unkind, and *that* — not the conspiracy, not the
chalkboard — is the real evidence in the whole film that Gary has a good mind. Sal
keeps a level his father gave him and has never once needed to.

## ROYCE — the future Gary cannot see

**Natal chart.** 30 November 1959. **Sagittarius sun** (the preacher, the horizon),
**Pisces moon** (the dreamer, the martyr), **Sun square Neptune** (the grand illusion
he sells and is the first to fall for). The prophet and the patsy in one chart.

**The wound (submerged).** He was a real associate professor once, in a real
department, and he published a thing, and the thing was wrong, and instead of taking
the correction he doubled, and tripled, and lost the post, the journals, the marriage,
the friends — who did not argue with him, who simply, gently, became *worried.* He
rebuilt himself out of the only material left: he decided the correction was the
cover-up. *(Deep:* even Royce no longer knows if he believes it. He needs Gary as
badly as Gary needs him — a disciple is proof you were never crazy — and that
symbiosis is the saddest engine in the film.)*

**The parts that go nowhere.** Pours coffee into a real cup and saucer, the last formal
object in a ruined room. Keeps every rejection letter in a box he calls "the file." Was,
by every account, a wonderful teacher, once.

---

## DONNA — the visa, unread

**Natal chart.** 21 July 1979. **Cancer sun** (the feeder, the keeper), **Virgo moon**
(the one who notices everything and says little), **Libra rising** (warmth as a trade).

**The life that goes nowhere.** Twelve years behind Delmore's counter; a marriage that
ended quietly; a son in the Army who calls on Sundays. She has watched Gary at the
same booth for years and likes both halves of him, the bright one and the decent one,
and has been patient past the point of dignity. *(Submerged:* years ago, before it got
bad, she found his theories charming and told him so, and she carries a private, unfair
fear that she helped wind the spring.)* She keeps his usual order memorized and brings
the pie whether he asks or not.

---

## RAY HALLORAN — the survivable disappointment

**Natal chart.** 3 October 1971. **Libra sun** (the manager, the keeper of the peace),
**Virgo moon** (the worrier with the clipboard), **Taurus rising**. Saturn on the moon:
the man who absorbs the building's anxiety so no one else has to.

**The rhyme (submerged).** He wanted to be an engineer. He is, instead, the man who
makes sure the engineers' building has heat. He made his peace with that around forty,
in a parking lot, alone, and it is the very peace Gary cannot make — which is why, of
everyone, Halloran is the one chained in the basement: he is the cautionary angel, the
ordinary disappointed life that *works,* and Gary has him gagged in a chair so he does
not have to look at it.

**The parts that go nowhere.** A wife, Carol, who thinks he is at a conference. Two kids
mid-tuition. A model railroad in the garage he tells no one about. A kindness that has
cost him his whole career and that he would not trade.

---

## PROFESSOR LIND — at peace with settling

**Natal chart.** 12 March 1964. **Pisces sun** (the gentle drift), **Sagittarius moon**
(the lecturer's ease), **Cancer rising**. A soft, late-career chart.

**The quiet truth (Deep).** He suspects, and has made his peace with suspecting, that he
was never going to be first-rate, that he is a good teacher and a forgettable
mathematician, and that this is, in the end, a fine thing to have been. He has erased
the "phantom's" scrawls off his board for months, mildly amused, and it has genuinely
never once crossed his mind that a man's entire soul is hanging on whether he reads them.
That blindness is not cruelty. It is just the ordinary distance between people, and in
this film it is fatal.

## THE WEAVE — the B-stories that grow from this

Each character is a different answer to the one question — *what do you do when you find
out you were never special* — and each answer is a B-story refracting Gary's A-story.

- **Donna (the love line):** the ordinary happiness on offer, held out and unread. Her
  patience running out is the clock on Gary's real life.
- **Sal (the brother line):** the good plain life (the Whitmore job, the crew) Gary
  keeps refusing. Sal's accommodation is *acceptance,* and it works.
- **Royce (the mirror line):** the same refusal as Gary's, ten years down the road. His
  accommodation is the *wrong* one — rebuild reality rather than accept it — and he is
  what Gary is becoming. The film's only foreshadow, and Gary cannot read it.
- **Halloran (the rhyme line):** disappointment *survived.* Gary literally imprisons it.
- **Lind (the trigger line):** disappointment at peace, so at peace it cannot even see
  the man it is destroying.

## METHOD NOTE — why the flesh comes from here

The bar scene is no longer "beat four." It now carries the manuscript in the drawer, the
charge Gary took at seventeen, the prints Sal can't read. Donna's booth carries twelve
years. Royce's cup and saucer carry a lost department. None of it gets *said.* It is
here so the people are people, and so every scene has more under it than the beat asked
for. The skeleton of this hippo is twenty-four beats. This is the animal.
