The Wall
To hold digital dollars, you have to hold a key.
Twelve words on a slip of paper. Lose them and the money is gone for good: no reset, no branch. Hand them to a company, and you have rebuilt the bank you were trying to skip. This is the wall the whole industry keeps hitting.
$12B+ stolen or scammed from crypto users in 2024  ·  $0 returned
Riften Labs03
Fifteen Years of Trying
Two ways to cross it. Both have failed.

Teach everyone

Seed-phrase drills and warning screens. Fifteen years on, self-custody is still a hobby for the technical.

Trust your circle

"Social recovery" is the right idea: the people you trust restore your key. But the circle rusts. Few can name five guardians who will show up in year three, and the first real attempt comes on the worst day of a life.

The cryptography holds. The humans rust.
Riften Labs04
What's Actually Missing
Social recovery has never had one thing: trust with a maintenance schedule.
A group that stays intact, and stays in practice, on a calendar. Find that, and self-custody stops being a hobby.
Riften Labs05
Fieldwork
It already exists. It's older than the bank.
Ask how you say "bank" in any language: banco, banque, banka, банк, बैंक. The same loanword everywhere the European bank arrived. The institution was imported. What was there first has a native name in every culture.
tanda · hui · susu · esusu · equb · gam'eya · arisan · pardner · tontine · chit · kameti
All one thing: a rotating savings and credit association.

The weekly meeting becomes a key ceremony; the members become the guardians. Once a cycle, one member wipes his phone and restores his wallet in the room, in front of everyone. The fire drill that makes recovery boring.
